About Us

Pioneered in 2008, DREAMHUB Unlimited Inc. is a humanitarian association working with, but not limited to, poor and marginalized people groups. It is a non-stock, non-profit, and inter-sectoral organization which forms partnerships with local government units, private non-government organizations and various sectors of society, whose focus is geared on mobilizing community development by uplifting the lives of people in the poverty and calamity stricken areas of the Philippines and other Asian countries.

The movers behind the group believe that it is not enough to simply feed, clothe, and provide shelter to the less privileged. Primary in their agenda is to educate, empower and inculcate the relevant values needed to manifest and breed the environment of self-sufficiency and self-reliance.

Presently, Dreamhub is actively involved in livelihood/entrepreneurial coaching classes in Kasiglahan Village, an urban resettlement village in Rodriguez, Rizal; basic and practical life values coaching classes among the Aetas tribe of Botolan, Zambales; and post-stress debriefing and next level community building classes for Typhoon "Yolanda" victims in Guiuan, Eastern Samar.

Dreamhub offers a series of lessons in Livelihood and Practical Financial Management and Accountability. Added to these are topics that reinforce values adapted to the socio-cultural context which are simple to understand, yet proven to be powerful and life changing. The organization also assists in providing small capital start-ups for trainees who are ready to engage in small livelihood projects, businesses and enterprises.

A moving force that continues to drive Dreamhub forward is it's uncanny ability to address the psycho-emotional issues and the socio-economic contexts of the people it caters to.

Stories

MARK LUMAGBAS

THE VULCANIZING SHOP OF HIS DREAMS

Mark, a 21-year old automotive technology graduate from the Eastern Samar University, attended Dream Hub’s “ ABKD ng Buhay” values training, a post-stress debriefing program which prepares calamity victims mentally and emotionally to begin livelihood programs.

The son of a fisherman without a steady source of income and a househelp employed in Tacloban, he is the second in a brood of eight where the household income averages Php 6,ooo (USD 130) a month. As the eldest son, he has since taken on odd jobs as tricycle driver, construction worker and errand boy to support his siblings. His dream is to have an automotive repair shop to help augment the family’s income.

Recently, a generous donor was able to sponsor the start-up capital he needed to set-up a vulcanizing shop, a stepping stone in achieving his dream of a full-service automotive center. Through the auspices of Dream Hub, money was sent through an authorized representative who then assisted Mark in his purchase of the much needed equipments and materials in Tacloban City.

To avoid high overhead costs, a family friend allowed him to use a small shed about an arm’s length in width as his business site. At last, he has set up his small vulcanizing shop along the main road in Barangay Cantahay, Guiuan, Eastern Samar. Even if the business is in its early stage, it has began to instill hope in Mark’s heart that the bright future that he has always dreamed of has finally begun.



Jessie and Norma

FREEZER x 2

Jessie and Norma are pastors of a local church in Guiuan, Eastern Samar, Philippines. Like most people in Guiuan, their main source of livelihood was their fishing boat. Unfortunately, their boat was completely destroyed after Typhoon Haiyan came and ravaged their town on November 8, 2013.

With no stable source of livelihood, they had to make do with the relief they received after the typhoon. With the help of kind donors, Jessie and Norma bought a freezer so they could produce ice which fishermen need to preserve their catch.

Soon enough, fishermen who bought their ice told them that out of all the ice dealers in the market, theirs was the slowest to melt. Word spread and suddenly, there was high demand for their slow melting ice. However, the fluctuating electricity due to unstable power lines did not help their cause as their sole freezer could not keep up with the demand for their ice. They resolved to look for an Auto Voltage Regulator (AVR) to help ease the fluctuation problems.

They came to a freezer store and inquired with the owner about the availability of an AVR. When the store owner learned that they are pastors who sell ice for the fishermen, the store owner gave them a special deal to buy a second unit freezer – with free delivery on the same day! The second freezer's arrival could not have been any more timely.

Today, Jessie and Norma are now supplying their slow melting ice to sixteen (16) fishing boats. The well-preserved catch in turn helps the fishermen get better prices, and this helps the Guiuan fishing community get back on their feet almost two years after Typhoon Haiyan struck them.
